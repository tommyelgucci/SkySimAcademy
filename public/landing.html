How the app is built

Four parts.
One app.

SkySimAcademy pairs short ground-school lessons with a real 3D simulator, so what you read is what you fly next.

Stage 1

Ground school, five modules.

Principles of flight, cockpit instruments, weather, radio & the phonetic alphabet, and basic navigation — three short lessons and a quiz per module, in all five languages.

Principles of flight Cockpit instruments Weather Radio & phonetic alphabet Navigation
Stage 2

A real exam, then a memory for your mistakes.

A 20-question mock exam at the real pace, plus a review mode that brings back exactly the questions you got wrong — in quizzes or the exam — until you clear them.

Mock exam Review mistakes
Stage 3

A 3D simulator that teaches by feel.

Arcade flight physics with real stall behaviour, a full instrument panel, day/dusk/night lighting, four scenarios, and an ILS-style landing evaluator that rates your touchdown.

Instrument panel Day / dusk / night 4 scenarios Landing rating
Stage 4

Flight school: levels, licences, missions.

Ten guided missions across four progressive levels — student pilot, basic instruments, navigation, emergencies — each one unlocking once you pass its matching theory module.

10 missions 4 levels Emergency drills

What "review mistakes" really means here

Not an autopilot.
A memory for your mistakes.

Every answer is saved. Questions you get wrong in a quiz or the mock exam come back in the review list — until you clear them, not just guess right once.

Not a marketing promise — it's the actual logic behind the review-mistakes screen: it decides what comes back next, not chance.
